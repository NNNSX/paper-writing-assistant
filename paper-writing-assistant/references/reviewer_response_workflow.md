# Reviewer Response Workflow

Use this reference for reviewer comments, rebuttals, response letters, revision summaries, and synchronized manuscript edits.

## Response Structure

Each response should close the loop:

```text
reviewer concern -> our understanding -> manuscript change or experiment -> result or clarification -> exact paper location -> quoted or summarized revised text
```

Do not answer only with `We have clarified this issue`.

## Tone

- Be professional, direct, and non-defensive.
- Acknowledge unclear original wording when relevant.
- Avoid victory language.
- Do not imply the reviewer failed to understand.
- Do not overstate what the new experiment or edit proves.

## Manuscript Synchronization

For every response item, check:

- Did the manuscript actually change?
- Are section, figure, table, equation, and appendix references correct?
- Do response-letter numbers match the final manuscript?
- Are captions, table notes, and method names synchronized?
- Are clean, tracked, response, supplement, and final PDF mutually consistent?

## Manuscript Body Rule

The paper body must not contain response-letter context such as:

- `following the reviewer's suggestion`;
- `as requested by the reviewer`;
- `in the revised version`;
- `additional experiment requested`;
- `we thank the reviewer`.

Convert the revision into natural paper narrative.

## Output Template

```text
Response to Reviewer X, Comment Y:

Response:
...

Manuscript revision:
- Location:
- Action:
- Revised text:

Synchronization check:
- Numbers verified:
- Figure/table/equation references verified:
- Remaining user verification:
```
